Machine Learning to Unlock Impact Investment in Latin America explores how data intelligence and machine learning can strengthen the allocation of impact capital in emerging markets. While the global impact investing market exceeds $1.5 trillion, only a small fraction of that capital reaches Latin American social enterprises, often due to information asymmetries, fragmented data, and high due-diligence costs. This project investigates how machine learning can help extract structured signals from sparse financial and impact data, model portfolio-level risk, and support decision infrastructure that connects investors with high-impact ventures. By building an “impact intelligence stack” — from signal extraction to capital activation — the research aims to make social enterprises in Latin America more visible, credible, and investable within global impact markets.
