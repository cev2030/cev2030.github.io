title: 'UChicago I-House Website Accessibility'
---

The I-House Website Accessibility Improvement Project addressed recurring accessibility issues across the International House website at the University of Chicago through a structured remediation process combining Siteimprove accessibility audits and manual HTML review. The project focused on resolving large-scale issues affecting more than 1,500 pages, including missing image alternative text, unlabeled buttons, links without accessible names, and decorative images incorrectly exposed to assistive technologies. Improvements were implemented through standardized HTML attribute corrections (alt, aria-label), screen-reader friendly labeling practices, and pattern-based remediation across WordPress and Divi site templates. In total, the project resolved more than 65,000 accessibility occurrences and improved the website’s accessibility compliance by 4.56 Siteimprove conformance points, while also producing a detailed technical report and documentation repository to support future accessibility maintenance and reviews.
